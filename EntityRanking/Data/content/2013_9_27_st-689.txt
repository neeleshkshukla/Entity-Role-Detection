Bombs ripped through outdoor markets in and near Baghdad on Thursday , killing at least 23 people and wounding dozens , the latest in a deadly wave that has hit Iraq in recent months . Three bombs went off simultaneously in the Shia village of Sabaa al-Bour , about 30 km north of the Iraqi capital . Police said the explosions two at the market entrance and one inside the shopping area went off as the place was packed with shoppers , killing 16 people and wounding 41 there . The attack came shortly after a bomb blast hit the al-Athorien market in Baghdads southern neighbourhood of Dora killing seven . AP OPEN
