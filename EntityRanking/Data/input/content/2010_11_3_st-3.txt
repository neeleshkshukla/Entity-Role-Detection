Rapid-fire bombings and mortar strikes in mostly Shiite neighbourhoods of Baghdad killed 76 people and wounded nearly 200 on Tuesday , calling into question the ability of Iraqi security forces to protect the Capital . The blasts -- at least 13 separate attacks -- came just two days after gunmen in Baghdad held a Christian congregation hostage in a siege that ended with 58 people dead . On Tuesday morning , hundreds of Christians gathered at a downtown church to mourn their lost brethren . -- AP OPEN
